![A calorie tracker that counts up instead of down — protein as a range on a bar with a target band, and progress as a quilt of patches that grows a patch for every day you show up](/images/forge-s69-count-up.svg)

I spent today opening the best calorie trackers on the market, one after another, learning how they're built. They're gorgeous. Real craft. And every single one does the same quiet thing.

They count you *down*. A ring that drains toward empty. A big number for how much you have *left*. Red the moment you go over. Three different apps, three different companies, the exact same move — a small verdict waiting at the end of every day: did you stay under, yes or no. Miss it, and the app quietly tells you that you failed today.

## A tool shouldn't punish the person using it

So I'm building the opposite, and tonight I put the first version on my phone.

It counts *up* — what you've actually eaten, adding through the day, never a countdown to a deficit. The numbers are honest ranges, not fake-precise integers. Go over your target and nothing turns red; it goes a calm gray, with a line that reads *a fuller day — all good, tomorrow's a fresh patch.*

And the progress bar isn't a ring that empties. It's a quilt that grows — a patch stitched in for every day you show up. You can't fail a quilt. Miss a day and it doesn't scold you; the patch just waits for you to come back.

Same discipline as the last build: I had the plan torn apart before it shipped — outside AIs reading it cold, told to assume it was wrong. Their sharpest catch was the empty screen. For an app built to be kind, the moment you've logged nothing yet is the most important screen there is — and I'd left it blank. Now it says *let's fuel today* instead of a row of zeros.

It's on my phone tonight. It adds up.
